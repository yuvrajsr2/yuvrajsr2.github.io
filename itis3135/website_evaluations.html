Website Evaluations

Dropbox

Dropbox is a file hosting service that offers cloud storage, file synchronization, personal cloud, and client software. It allows users to store and share files and folders with others across the internet using cloud technology. Dropbox provides a convenient way to back up important files, collaborate on projects, and access files from multiple devices.

  • Contrast: Dropbox uses plenty of whitespace with blue accents to guide attention to buttons and links.
  • Repetition: Fonts, button styles, and color schemes are consistent across all pages, reinforcing a professional identity.
  • Alignments: Everything on the page follows a grid system. Headings, text, and images are well-aligned.
  • Proximity: Related content, such as a headline and its call-to-action button, are grouped closely together for readibility

Arngren.net

Arngren.net is overwhelming and disorienting, with a barrage of images, ads, and cluttered layout. It’s a textbook example of poor design practices taken to the extreme.

  • Contrast: Too many contrasting elements competing for attention — nothing stands out neatly.
  • Repetition: No consistent styling — fonts, colors, and layouts shift erratically.
  • Alignment: Elements are misaligned, placed seemingly at random, lacking any grid structure.
  • Proximity: Items that have nothing to do with each other are crammed together, making it hard to find relevant info.
